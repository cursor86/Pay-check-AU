The 12% Superannuation Increase: A Guide for Australian Workers

On July 1, 2025, the Australian workforce reached a historic milestone: the Superannuation Guarantee (SG) increased to 12%. This change marks the culmination of a decade-long plan to increase retirement savings for millions of Australians. However, while the increase is a win for your "future self," it can have immediate implications for your current take-home pay depending on how your employment contract is structured.

"Plus Super" vs. "Total Package": Why It Matters

Most Australian workers fall into one of two categories. If your contract is Base Plus Super, your employer must now pay 12% on top of your agreed salary. In this scenario, your take-home pay remains unchanged, but your retirement balance grows faster. If your contract is Total Remuneration Package (TRP), the 12% super is deducted from your total salary. This means as super goes up, your taxable cash income may actually decrease slightly. Our calculator above helps you toggle between these two views to see exactly where your money is going.

Understanding the Stage 3 Tax Cuts in 2025-26

The 2025-26 financial year is the first full year where Australians are feeling the total impact of the restructured Stage 3 Tax Cuts. The most significant change was the introduction of the 16% tax bracket for income between $18,201 and $45,000. Previously, this income was taxed at 19%, meaning lower and middle-income earners now see more cash in their bank accounts every fortnight. For those earning between $45,000 and $135,000, the rate remains at 30%, which is a significant drop from the previous 32.5% or 37% rates that applied to those bands.

The New Marginal HECS/HELP Repayment System

Starting in 2025, the Australian government transitioned student loan repayments (HECS/HELP) to a marginal repayment system. Under the old rules, if you earned just one dollar over a threshold, you were required to pay a percentage of your entire income, creating a "tax cliff." The new system ensures you only pay a percentage on the dollars earned above the threshold ($67,000 for 2025-26). This is a much fairer system that prevents graduates from being penalised for taking on extra shifts or receiving small pay rises.

Optimising Your Pay: Salary Sacrifice and Deductions

With the tax rates lower than in previous decades, 2025-26 is an excellent time to consider salary sacrificing. By contributing additional funds to your super or using a novated lease for an EV (which remains Fringe Benefit Tax exempt), you can lower your taxable income. However, because the Medicare Levy remains at 2% for most earners, it is vital to calculate your "adjusted taxable income" to ensure you don't accidentally trigger the Medicare Levy Surcharge if you don't have private health insurance and earn over $97,000 as a single.
