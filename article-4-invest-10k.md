## Step 1: Before You Invest Anything

Before you allocate a single dollar to the market, make sure:

- You have an emergency fund (3–6 months of living expenses) in a high-interest savings account
- You have no high-interest debt (credit cards, personal loans above 8–10%)
- You have adequate insurance (life, income protection, health)

Investing $10,000 while carrying $5,000 in credit card debt at 20% interest is a losing strategy. Pay off high-interest debt first — it's the highest guaranteed return you'll get.

If those boxes are ticked, you're ready to invest.

## The 2026 Investment Environment

Australia's investment landscape in 2026 has a few defining characteristics:

**Interest rates:** The RBA has moved through a rate-cutting cycle. Term deposit rates have eased from their 2023–24 peaks but still offer reasonable returns of 4–4.5% for 12-month terms at major banks.

**ASX:** The Australian sharemarket has continued to reward long-term investors. Resources, financials, and infrastructure have been standout sectors. Broad-market ETFs remain the most reliable vehicle for retail investors.

**Global equities:** US tech and AI-infrastructure stocks have driven strong returns in the NASDAQ 100, though valuations remain elevated. Diversification into international markets is still highly recommended.

**Crypto:** Bitcoin has matured as an asset class, with institutional adoption increasing. However, volatility remains high and ATO compliance requirements are non-negotiable for Australian investors.

This is not a one-size-fits-all model — your age, risk tolerance, timeline, and tax situation all matter. But as a starting framework for a 30-something Australian investor with a 10+ year horizon:

VDHG is a single-fund solution that holds approximately 90% growth assets (Australian and international shares) and 10% defensive assets (bonds). It's diversified across thousands of companies globally, automatically rebalanced, and has a low management fee of 0.27% per year.

For most retail investors, VDHG alone is a perfectly adequate long-term investment. It removes the need to manage multiple ETFs and eliminates the temptation to tinker.

*Buy on the ASX through any Australian broker (CommSec, Stake, Superhero, SelfWealth).*

### $2,000 — Stability and Income (20%)
**High-yield savings account or term deposit**

Park $2,000 in a term deposit or high-yield savings account earning 4–4.5%. This provides:
- A liquid buffer if you need cash unexpectedly
- A stable, predictable return while you wait for better entry points in the market
- Peace of mind that not everything is at market risk

Compare rates at RateCity or Canstar before committing.

### $2,000 — International Growth (20%)
**BetaShares NASDAQ 100 ETF (NDQ)**

NDQ gives you exposure to the 100 largest non-financial companies listed on the NASDAQ — including Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Alphabet, and Amazon. It's a concentrated bet on US technology and innovation, which has driven outsized returns over the past decade.

This allocation adds meaningful international diversification and growth potential. Be aware it's more volatile than VDHG and is priced in USD — you have currency exposure.

### $1,000 — Speculative (10%)
**Bitcoin or other digital assets**

A small speculative allocation — 10% — gives you exposure to asymmetric upside without betting the house. Bitcoin is the most established digital asset and the most liquid.

**Critical Australian tax note:** Every time you sell, swap, or spend cryptocurrency in Australia, it's a taxable event for Capital Gains Tax (CGT) purposes. The ATO uses data-matching with Australian exchanges. Keep records of every transaction.

If you hold Bitcoin for more than 12 months before selling, you're eligible for a 50% CGT discount on any capital gain — significantly reducing your tax liability.

## Australian Tax Considerations for Investors

### Capital Gains Tax (CGT)
When you sell an investment for more than you paid, you make a capital gain — and it's added to your taxable income for that year. If you hold the investment for more than 12 months first, you get a 50% discount on the gain.

**Example:** You buy $5,000 of VDHG. It grows to $7,000 and you sell after 14 months. Your capital gain is $2,000. With the 50% discount, only $1,000 is added to your taxable income.

### Dividends and Franking Credits
Australian shares often pay fully franked dividends — meaning the company has already paid 30% corporate tax on the profits. You receive a franking credit attached to the dividend, which you can use to offset your personal tax. For investors in lower tax brackets, this can result in a tax refund.

VDHG distributes dividends quarterly that include both Australian and international components — check your tax statement for the franking credit breakdown.

### Keep Records
The ATO expects you to keep records of every investment purchase and sale — the date, price, brokerage fees, and any other costs. Brokerage fees increase your cost base and reduce your capital gain. Most modern brokers (Stake, CommSec) make this easy by providing annual tax statements.

## The Single Most Important Rule

**Time in the market beats timing the market.**

Trying to wait for the "perfect" entry point is a strategy that reliably underperforms simply investing and holding. Market pullbacks feel terrible in the moment but are normal — and historically, every Australian market downturn has eventually been followed by new highs.

Invest regularly, don't panic-sell, and let compounding do the heavy lifting.
